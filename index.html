About

I'm a second-year Cognitive Neuroscience MSc student in Budapest, where I finished first in my cohort last semester. I also work as a Software Engineer at Voovo.

My main interest is mechanistic interpretability — understanding what representations neural networks actually learn, and whether we can make that legible. I'm particularly drawn to applying interpretability tools (sparse autoencoders, probing, attention analysis) to vision-language models, and to questions at the boundary of cognitive science and AI.

I'm also interested in how strategic reasoning works — in games, in competition, and in complex systems. Sometimes I write about that too.
